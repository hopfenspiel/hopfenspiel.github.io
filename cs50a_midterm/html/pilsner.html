Pilsner Aus Pilsen

Pilsner, from Pilsen

First the Czech Republic, then the World!

Before pilsner became the world's most ubiquitous style of beer, it found its humble beginnings in the then-Austro-Hungarian Empire
(now-Czech-Republic) small town of Pilsen (or Plzen), hence the name pilsner (in German, of or from Pilsen).

Known for its crisp, clean flavor, it's a surprisingly difficult beer to produce. Any inconsistencies in the brewing process will be
apparent in the final product, meaning that the brewer responsible for its production must truly be a master. Though widely taken for
granted by the drinking public, a good pilsner is a brewer's biggest source of pride (and, potentially, agony).
